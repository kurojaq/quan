# What it is

The final and most unusual document in the source corpus — explicitly framed as *"not a psychological supplement... the sustained attempt to follow one concept, Intent, the raw material of CW = −1, as far as serious philosophical thought has taken it, and then to return with something usable at CW = 0."* It's presented as an extension of something called **"Psyq"** — referenced constantly throughout [PRAQ](/analytics/praq-mission-discipline.md) as the psychological-architecture pillar (internal "Hunters," the reset protocol, Hunter states, Frankl Space, Decreation Quality) — but **Psyq's own content was never located as a standalone document anywhere in this source file**; it exists only by reference. Treat any Psyq-specific claim in this concept as inherited context, not confirmed doctrine.

**Terminal proposition**: *"Intent is not a mental event that precedes action. It is a field — the entire structure of attention, endorsement, auto-affection, narratively grounded self-constitution, and vital engagement that makes an action genuinely the practitioner's own rather than a reaction that happens to use the execution interface."*

# The Four Grades of Genuineness

A self-diagnostic scale for whether a Mission Brief is actually "intended" or merely produced:

- **Grade I — Functional (Anscombe)**: entry/stop/target/abort are all specified — the minimum for calling it a Mission Brief at all. **Not sufficient**: it could have been generated by an algorithm, and holds only until the first real Hunter-state pressure, because nothing genuinely endorsed it.
- **Grade II — Endorsed (Frankfurt, Korsgaard)**: the practitioner's second-order volition actively endorses the trade — no ambivalence. Survives moderate pressure because the endorsement, not just the specification, is real.
- **Grade III — Attentive (Murdoch, Weil, James, Husserl)**: formed through genuinely attentive, non-category-confirming engagement with the field's concrete structure — a wide, carefully-populated horizon. Survives substantial pressure because the practitioner can evaluate a Hunter's claim ("this is wrong") against direct concrete perception rather than relying on the Brief's authority alone.
- **Grade IV — Living (Henry, Whitehead, Bergson, Fichte)**: a living act of self-constitution — vitally present, auto-affectively engaged, striving against resistance in the very act of forming the Brief. Doesn't just survive Hunter-state pressure, it transforms it — execution is the unfolding of a process that began in pre-session silence, not a decision made under pressure. Framed as equivalent to "Musashi's Void" — rare, temporary, recognized retroactively in the AAR, not a technique to be applied on demand.

**Diagnostic standards** drawn from specific chapters (usable as literal pre-Brief checklist questions): *Husserl's Challenge* — can you describe the intentional horizon of your primary PDSL, not just its own observables? *Anscombe's Test* — can every execution action be referred to a description in the Brief, or is it "a reaction that happens to involve the execution interface"? *Frankfurt's Standard* — are you wholehearted, or is unresolved ambivalence present? *Murdoch's Challenge* — did anything in the IPS Layer-1 read contradict your prior expectation (if nothing surprised you, you may have been attending to a model of the field rather than the field)? *Korsgaard's Standard* — do you genuinely endorse the Brief, or is it "a document" without normative authority at CW=0?

# Interpretation

This document is explicitly a **psychological/discipline layer**, not a source of new computable trading signals — it reframes existing PRAQ mechanics (the IPS, the Mission Brief, the Selection Standard, the reset protocol) rather than adding new formulas. Its practical yield is the Four Grades scale, usable as an honest post-hoc AAR question ("which grade was this Brief actually operating at?") rather than a pre-trade gate like Risq or the Deep Strike Scorecard.
