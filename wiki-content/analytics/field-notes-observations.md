# What it is

The source frames these explicitly as *"not procedures... the kind that only become visible after sitting long enough with a framework to notice what it does not say about itself... offered not as doctrine but as a lens."* Ten observations, each with a stated execution consequence.

# The observations

1. **Mass creates the smoothness that makes the trade possible.** High-[Mass](/analytics/strike-observable-manifold.md) strikes geometrically coincide with low-[Jerk](/analytics/strike-observable-manifold.md) — concentrated, predictable dealer obligation produces low-dimensional, ordered microstructure. Tick-chart Elliott Wave counting is only structurally valid near high-Mass strikes. **Check Jerk at every candidate PDSL, always** — a Mass>2.0/Jerk>100 strike is an internal contradiction the scorecard alone won't surface.
2. **The negative CW arc speaks forward, not backward.** Toroidal motion means the negative arc is preparation, not history — a zero-cross at CW=−0.7 is a precondition for what the positive arc will express, not a record of what happened. The highest-information readings are the quiet ones at CW=−0.5/−0.3, systematically underweighted because they're undramatic. Add them as explicit pre-session checkpoints even when they appear to carry no signal — apparent silence there predicts a lower-volatility positive arc.
3. **Entropy is the most honest signal in the framework.** Counterintuitively, **high-entropy sessions tend to produce the most reliable next-session setups** — the session discharged its accumulated structural tension, leaving a lower-energy, more orderly regime behind. A low-entropy session (compression without release) is the one to approach the next day with more caution, not less. Track the entropy trend (rising/falling) across the last three sessions, not just the current reading.
4. **The pending order is a structural claim, not a directional bet.** A limit order at a PDSL claims a *mechanical* relationship (dealer delta-hedging will generate a tradeable departure), not a price prediction. This reframes what counts as a valid cancellation reason: adverse price movement toward the order is **not** invalidating — it's the setup approaching. The claim fails only when Mass at the PDSL drops below 1.5 before fill, a ZC flag fires in the CW quadrant immediately preceding the fill zone, or ICF Time Density at the PDSL starts falling on approach.
5. **The three speeds (SoI/SoT/SoR) define a predictive window — and it closes.** The window is open while Intent (DIDK high) has no corresponding Realization (DR3 low); it closes when DR3 rises to meet DID. This is a function of dealer hedging throughput, not clock time — check DR3 at the PDSL throughout the session; DR3>0.6 with Layer A filled but Layer B unconfirmed means execute immediately or abandon, not wait for ideal conditions. Highest-conviction window: roughly CW=−0.5 to +0.3.
6. **The stop is a statement about the structure, not the position.** Correct placement is just beyond the structural invalidation boundary — a stop closer than that fires before the structural claim has been tested, which is "disbelieving your own analysis at full-conviction prices for a half-conviction position." The structural stop distance should determine position size, never the reverse — if the honest stop is 15 ticks and only 8 is tolerable, size down or skip the trade; do not move the stop.
7. **Line and candlestick charts are complementary, not competing.** Line charts (committed closes) are correct for Elliott Wave counting — they strip intrabar indecision. Candlesticks reveal *rejection* (wick vs. body) — a long upper wick at a PDSL shows dealer selling in real time that a line chart's flat close would hide entirely. Operational sequence: count the μ-Wave on the line chart, then confirm on the candlestick that the μ-Wave-2 low shows a wick rejection before sizing Layer B at full conviction.
8. **The Fibonacci between strikes describes sub-obligations.** Fibonacci levels drawn PDSL-to-PDSL (rather than swing-high-to-low) tend to land on smaller structural obligations elsewhere in the chain, because dealer hedging ladders are themselves distributed in patterns Fibonacci ratios happen to capture. After drawing the strike Fibonacci, scan the 0.382/0.500/0.618 price levels for Mass — a level landing on a strike with Mass>0.5 is a confirmed (higher-conviction) level; one landing between strikes is purely geometric.
9. **The conductance gate is a temporal humility requirement, not just a sizing modifier.** Low conductance doesn't mean "the market is dangerous" — it means *your reading of it* is less reliable, an epistemological problem, not a risk one. In a low-conductance session, honor the stop without hesitation, since ambiguity about whether the analysis is even valid removes the basis for tolerating adverse movement. Low conductance clusters across sessions — apply a 20% prior conviction reduction to the next session if `condFactor < 0.50`, absent a clean low-entropy close.
10. **The framework asks you to change your relationship with waiting.** Waiting is the primary activity, not the absence of trading — every CW increment in the negative arc is a required analytical task (has DIPLTR changed? new ZC flags? is ICF Time Density rising or falling at the PDSL?), not empty time. A session where everything is observed and nothing is traded is not wasted — it is what makes the next session's execution precise.
